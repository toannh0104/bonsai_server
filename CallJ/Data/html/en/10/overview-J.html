Using the "て" form of the verb plus "いる" to express states and progressive actions

In this lesson we will practise making statements about states and progressive actions. These sentences use the "て" form of the verb plus the auxiliary verb "いる", and are similar to the pattern "be doing" in English.

We will also considering making sentences in both polite and plain forms. Two main forms to be practised in this lesson can be summarised by the following examples.

1Polite vs Plain:
父は新聞を読んでいますMy father is reading the newspaper.
父は新聞を読んでいるMy father is reading the newspaper.
2Question vs Statement:
サリーさんは皿を洗っていますかIs Sally washing the dishes?
サリーさんは皿を洗っていますSally is washing the dishes.
* Note: All answers given in this lesson should be of the form(s) shown above.
