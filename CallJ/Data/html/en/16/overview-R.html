"If it is sunny I will go"
Conditional statements

In this lesson we will practise sentences with conditional clauses. We will look at various phrases which can be used to mark a clause as conditional and attach it to a sentence, and the different nuances that these words carry. In this lesson you will be given two pictures, one depicting the conditional clause and the other depicting the sentence, and asked to choose the the correct grammar pattern to connect them. The main forms to be practised in this lesson are summarised by the following examples.

1Conditional assumptions
hima nara kyoukai he ikouIf you are free, let's go to the church.
asu hima nara ginkou ni ikimasuIf I am free tomorrow, I will go to the bank.
2Simple conditionals
ame ga hut tara dekakenaiIf it rains, we won't go out.
shukudai ga owat tara asobouWhen we finish the homework, let's go play outdoors.
3Uncontrollable events
haru ni naru to atatakaku naruWhen Spring comes, it becomes warm.
aki ni naru to suzushiku naruWhen Autumn comes, it becomes cool.
4General conditionals
byouin made aruke ba tikai.The hospital is close if you walk.
toshokan made aruke ba tooi.The library is far if you walk.
* Note: All answers given in this lesson should be of the form(s) shown above.
