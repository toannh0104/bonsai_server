Introducing verbs and further particles to create simple statements about an action.
In this lesson we will practise making sentences by using various verbs. We will look at making sentences in both polite and plain form, using past and present tense, and with positive and negative verb forms. This lesson will also allow you to practice a number of various particles such as を (the object marker) and に・へ (direction markers). The forms you will be expected to know to complete this lesson are summarised below:
1Statement vs Question:
私はテレビを見ます"I will watch TV."
テレビを見ますか"Will you watch TV?"
2Positive vs Negative:
鈴木さんは来ます"Suzuki will come."
鈴木さんは来ません"Suzuki will not come."
3Non-Past vs Past:
私は今日東京へ行きます"I will go to Tokyo today."
私は昨日東京へ行きました"I went to Tokyo yesterday."
4Polite vs Plain:
私は手紙を書きます"I will write a letter."
私は手紙を書く"I will write a letter."
私は手紙を書きました"I wrote a letter."
私は手紙を書いた"I wrote a letter."
* Note: All answers given in this lesson should be of the form(s) shown above.
