Introducing verbs and further particles to create simple statements about an action.
In this lesson we will practise making sentences by using various verbs. We will look at making sentences in both polite and plain form, using past and present tense, and with positive and negative verb forms. This lesson will also allow you to practice a number of various particles such as Ç (the object marker) and Ç…ÅEÇ÷ (direction markers). The forms you will be expected to know to complete this lesson are summarised below:

1Statement vs Question:
watashi ha terebi wo mimasu"I will watch TV."
terebi wo mimasuka"Will you watch TV?"
2Positive vs Negative:
suzukisan ha kimasu"Suzuki will come."
suzukisan ha kimasen"Suzuki will not come."
3Non-Past vs Past:
watashi ha kyou toukyou he ikimasu"I will go to Tokyo today."
watashi ha kinou toukyou he ikimashita"I went to Tokyo yesterday."
4Polite vs Plain:
watashi ha tegami wo kakimasu"I will write a letter."
watashi ha tegami wo kaku"I will write a letter."
watashi ha tegami wo kakimashita"I wrote a letter."
watashi ha tegami wo kaita"I wrote a letter."
* Note: All answers given in this lesson should be of the form(s) shown above.
