Key Forms:

The following examples show the key forms to be practised in this lesson.

1Statement vs Request
私は切手を2枚 買いました"I bought two stamps."
切手を2枚 ください"Two stamps, please."
2Polite vs Plain
トムさんはエンピツを2本買いました"Tom bought two pencils." (polite)
トムさんはエンピツを2本買った"Tom bought two pencils." (plain)
3Non-past vs Past
ビールを2本買います"I will buy two beers." (non-past)
ビールを2本買いました"I bought two beers." (past)

The sentences you create in this lesson should all be similar to the forms shown above. The grammar required to form these sentences is covered in the text below.



Grammar Point 1: Counters

Counters are suffixes that are used when counting objects in Japanese. In general, the size and shape of an object determines what kind of counter is used. Some of the most common counters, which appear in this lesson, are shown below. Note that the pronunciation of the counter may vary depending on the quantity of the object.

Counter Type of Object Examples
個 Solid or round objects Apples, oranges, eggs
枚 Flat objects Sheets of paper, pizzas
本 Long, thin objects Umbrellas, pens, pencils, bottled drinks
杯 Cups of liquid Cups of coffee
冊 Books or magazines Books or magazines
足 Pairs of footwear, socks Pairs of socks or shoes

Here are some examples using different counters.

ペンを2本買いました - "I bought two pens." (polite)

鈴木さんは雑誌を3冊買った - "Suzuki bought three magazines." (plain)

ピザを1枚ください - "One pizza, please."

For quantities from one to ten, the traditional numerals given below can often be used if the correct counter is not known or no counter exists. However, whenever possible you should use the correct counter.

1.2 Simple requests using counters

We have already learnt how to make a simple request for an object with ください in an earlier lesson. The same basic pattern can be used with counters to indicate the quantity of the object.

エンピツをください - "A pencil, please."

エンピツを2本ください - "Two pencils, please."

リンゴをください - "An apple, please."

リンゴを3個ください - "Three apples, please."

To specify the quantity of the object, simply indicate the number with the appropriate counter after を and before ください . Requests for different kinds of items can also be made by connecting sentences with と as in the following example.

リンゴを3個とバナナを一本ください - "Three apples and one banana, please."
