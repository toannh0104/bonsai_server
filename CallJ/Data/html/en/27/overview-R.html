"I decided to see it"
Decisions


In this lesson we will practise how to express one's decision to do something. This grammar pattern is used with volitional actions performed by the speaker. We will also learn a related expression used when the speaker performs an action due to arrangements or events outside of his control. The choice between these two expressions to use is determined by whether or not the speaker made a voluntary decision. See the example sentences below for examples.
1Speaker's positive decision (suru)
shatsu wo kau koto ni suruI've decided to buy a shirt.
ko-ra wo nomu koto ni shimashitaI decided to drink cola.
2Speaker's negative decision (suru)
terebi wo minai koto ni sitaI decided not to watch TV.
manga wo yomanai koto ni sitaI decided not to read comics.
3Someone else's positive decision (naru)
nihon de eigo wo osieru koto ni narimasitaIt's been decided that I'll teach English in Japan.
kyoto ni hikkosu koto ni nattaIt's been arranged that I'll move to Kyoto.
4Someone else's negative decision (naru)
terebi wo minai koto ni sitaIt's been decided that I won't go on vacation next month
manga wo yomanai koto ni sitaIt's been decided that I won't go to Tokyo next year.
