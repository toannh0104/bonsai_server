Grammar Point 1: The Causative Form

The causative form of verbs is used to show that someone makes or allows someone else to do something. First, we will study the causative form a sentence which has a transitive verb (one that takes a direct object). The person who causes the other person to take an action is marked with は or が , the person caused to perform the action is marked by に , the direct object is marked with を , and the verb is changed to the causative form.

Study the examples below.

先生がジョンに本を読ませました。"The professor made John read the book."
母は私にお皿を洗わせました。"My mother forced me to do the dishes."

The causer, marked with は or が , is the subject of the sentence and thus may be dropped when understood from context.

みちこにピアノを練習させました。 "I made Michiko practise the piano."

For intransitive verbs (ones that do not take direct objects), the person who is forced or allowed to perform an action is marked by を or に . The choice between these two particles is determined by whether or not the action performed was intentional or not. In general, に is used for volitional actions and has the nuance of permission, while を does not have this meaning and can be used irregardless of the volition of the person caused to act. However, this rule is not hard and fast and the context of the sentence will determine whether the nuance is one of persuasion or permission.

母はむりやりに姉をイギリスへ行かせました。"My mother forced my older sister to go to England." (coercion)
姉はイギリスに行ってみたいと言っていたから、母は彼女をイギリスに行かせました。 "My sister said she wanted to go to England, so my mother allowed her to go." (permission)

In the first example, 姉 must be marked with を because the speaker's older sister does not want to go. However, in the second example both に and を can be used to mark 彼女 because the speaker's older sister wanted to go.

In the case of transitive verbs, に is used to mark the person made to perform the action even if they do not want to perform the action. This is because を already appears in the clause to mark the direct object. Thus, both examples below use に even though the nuances are different.

私は弟に野菜を食べさせました。"I made (or let) my little brother eat vegetables." (permission)
私はむりやりに弟に野菜を食べさせました。"I forced by my little brother to eat vegetables." (coercion)

Grammar Point 2: Permission with the causative form

While the causative form is often used when one person is forced to do something by someone else, it can also be used when one person allows another to do something. This meaning of the causative form can also be used to make requests for permission to do something, similar to the expression "Could you let me..." in English. Study the examples below.

私にもプールに行かせて下さい。"Please let me go to the pool, too."
今日は休ませて下さい。"Please let me take today off."
弟にアイスを食べさせて下さい。"Please let my little brother eat some ice cream."
