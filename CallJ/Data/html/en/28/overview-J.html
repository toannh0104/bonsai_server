Compulsion, permission, and requests with the causative form


In this lesson we will look at the construction and use of the causative form. This is typically used to convey the meaning of compulsion, i.e. someone being compelled to do something. However, the same form may also be used to convey someone being allowed to do a particular action. Also, combined with 「ください」 (please), it may be used to ask permission to do an action.

1Type
私は子供に野菜を食べさせました"I made the child eat vegetables" (compulsion)
私は子供におかしを食べさせました"I allowed the child to eat sweets" (permission)
体が悪いので、早く帰らせてください"I feel bad, so please let me go home early" (request)
2Statement vs Question
教授は学生にレポートを書かせます"The professor makes the students to write a report" (Statement)
教授は学生にレポートを書かせますか"Does the professor make the students to write a report?"(Question)
3Non-past vs Past
部長は田中さんに東京へ行かせます"The director makes tanakasan to go to Tokyo"(non-past)
部長は田中さんに東京へ行かせました"The director made tanakasan to go to Tokyo" (past)
4Polite vs Plain
私は妹に勉強させます "I make my sister study"(polite)
私は妹に勉強させる"I make my sister study" (plain)
