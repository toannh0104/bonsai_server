Key Forms:

The following examples show the key forms to be practised in this lesson.

1Animate
jonsan wa ginkou ni imasu"John is at the bank."(Statement)
jonsan wa ginkou ni imasu ka"Is John at the bank?"(Question)
jonsan wa ginkou ni imasen"John isn't at the bank."(Negative)
jonsan wa ginkou ni iru"John is at the bank."(Plain)

The sentences you create in this lesson should all be similar to the forms shown above. The grammar required to form these sentences is covered in the text below.



Grammar Point 1: Describing the position of people (or animate creatures) and objects

Japanese uses different verbs to express the existence of animate creatures (like people or animals) and inanimate objects (such as a chair or book). The verb "iru" is used for the former case, and the verb "aru" is used for the latter case.

TypePlainPolite
Animate, Present, Positiveいるいます
Animate, Present, Negativeいないいません
Animate, Past, Positiveいたいました
Animate, Past, Negativeいなかったいませんでした
Inanimate, Present, Positiveあるあります
Inanimate, Present, Negativeないありません
Inanimate, Past, Positiveあったありました
Inanimate, Past, Negativeなかったありませんでした

Note that this distinction is only made in sentences expressing the concept of existence, not for sentences describing the subject with "desu" or sentences that use other verbs. This should be clear from the following Examples:

These sentences express existence, so we must use the appropriate verb.

Animate
今ジョンさんはいません - "John is not in Kyoto now."
あの公園にに犬がいます - "There is a dog in that park."
Inanimate
この街に映画館があります - "There is a cinema in this city."

"Desu" is used to describe both people (or animate creatures) and inanimate objects.

田中さんは学生です - "Tanaka is a student."

このビルは銀行です - "This building is a bank."

Most other verbs, for example "kuru" (to come or arrive), can be used for both people and inanimate objects.

バスが来ました - "The bus has arrived."

ジョンさんが来ました - "John has arrived.

Grammar Point 2: Relative Positions

Sentences used to describe the relative position of a person or object are very similar to sentences which express whether the subject exists. The only difference is that a prepositional phrase is inserted after the subject marked with "ha" or "ga". The prepositional phrases we will consider in this lesson consist of a noun followed by the particle "no" plus a preposition.

Prepositions
Xの上に - "under / underneath / below the X"
Xの下に - "on / on top of / above the X"
Xの前に - "in front of the X"
Xの中に - "in / inside / the X"
Xの横に - "next to / beside / the X"

Examples

鉛筆はベッドの下にあります - "The pencil is under the bed."

雑誌はテーブルの上にありません - "The magazine is not on top of the table."
