Grammar Point 1: Honorific forms

The honorific form is used when the subject of a sentence is a superior or has higher social status than the speaker. Using the correct honorific form shows respect for the subject. For most verbs, the honorific form is お plus the stem form of the verb plus になる . This honorific form is conjugated just like any other expression using the verb なる . Study the examples below.

来週先生はお休みになります。 "The professor will go on vacation next week."

社長は車をお買いになるそうです。 "I heard that the company president will buy a car."

2.1 Irregular honorific forms

While the honorific form for most verbs can be made with the pattern described above, the most common verbs have special honorific versions that must be learnt. The table below shows the main honorific forms that will be covered in this lesson. Note that all of the honorific verbs in this table are group 1 verbs.

Grammar Point 3: Modest (humble) forms

The modest (or humble) form is used for describing the actions of oneself or one's peers when speaking to a superior. It indirectly shows respect by lowering the position of the speaker. The construction for the humble form is お followed by the stem of the verb followed by する . To conjugate verbs in this grammar pattern, simply conjugate the final する .

Verbs which take the regular honorific form also take the regular modest form. Note that this grammar pattern is only used when the speaker's actions involve or affect a superior, and thus cannot be used in all situations. (See grammar point 3.2 below for exceptions.) Study the examples below.

先生に手紙をお送りします。 "I will send a letter to the professor."

お客さんとお話します。 "I will speak with the client."

Verbs consisting of a noun plus suru, such as 勉強する , have a different modest form. In this case, one of two special prefixes indicating politeness is added to the noun. In general, the prefix お is used for words of Japanese origin, while the prefix ご is used for kanji words of Chinese origin. However, お is used for some some common words (such as 勉強 , 掃除 and 電話) so the correct prefix must be learnt for these.

明日お電話します。 "I will call tomorrow."

来週またご連絡します。 "I will contact you again next week."

今週中に書類をご送付します。 "I will send the documents by the end of this week."
