Introduction to adjectives

In this lesson we will practise making simple statements about objects, conveying simple information about the object via the use of adjectives. Two forms of adjective use will be practiced. First, we consider the standard usage where the adjective is located after the noun in question. We also look at the case where an adjective modifies the noun that directly follows it. Finally, this lesson also introduces a further form of the definitive that is used to modify a noun.

1Standard vs Modifier:
Kono ke-ki ha oishii desu"This cake is tasty." (standard)
Kore ha oishii ke-ki desu"This is a tasty cake." (modifier)
2Question vs Statement:
Kore ha takai baggu desu"This is an expensive bag."
Kore ha takai baggu desu ka?"Is this an expensive bag?"
3Positive vs Negative:
Kono ko-hi- ha atsui desu"This coffee is hot."
Kono ko-hi- ha atsukunai desu"This coffee is not hot."
4Expressing "Also":
Kono bi-ru mo tsumetai desu"This beer is also cold."
