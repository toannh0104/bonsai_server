Grammar Point 1: Expressing feelings about objects and activities

The "na" adjective 好き is used to express one's positive feelings about an object or activity. It is equivalent to the expressions "to enjoy" or "to like" in English. In this lesson, the suki1 form is just the standard form for "na" adjectives that you practised in the previous lesson.

Positive
私はオレンジジュースが好きです - "I like orange juice."
Negative
私はコーラが好きではありません - "I don't like cola."

The example sentences above express feelings about a noun, so the adjective modifies the noun in question. A similar pattern that we call the suki2 form can be used for expressing feelings about an activity, but in this case the adjective modifies a simple sentence consisting of a verb followed by the marker の instead of a noun. The only difference between this form and the suki1 form is the phrase modified by the adjective, so the forms of the adjective itself are exactly the same.

Positive
私はコーヒーを飲むのが好きです - "I like drinking coffee."
Negative
私は勉強するのが好きではありません - "I don't like studying."

Grammar Point 2: Using adjectives as adverbs to express changes in size or number

The examples below show an expression used to indicate that the size or quantity of an object (described by an adjective) has changed. This expression consists of an "i" adjective describing size or quantity followed by the verb なる ("to become"). To create the negative form of this pattern, we simply use the negative past form of the verb.

Positive, Past
魚は大きくなりました - "The fish became bigger."
リンゴは多くなりました - "The apples became numerous." (The number of apples increased.)
Negative, Past
魚は大きくなりませんでした - "The fish didn't become bigger."
リンゴは多くなりませんでした - "The apples didn't become more numerous." (The number of apples didn't increase.)

This pattern is actually an example of an adjective being used as an adverb to modify a verb. When an adjective is used in this way, the final "i" sound changes to "ku" and the verb that is modified directly follows the adjective. We will only consider the following adjectives in this lesson, but in general any "i" adjective can be used.

Adjectives
大きい - big / large
多い - numerous / many
