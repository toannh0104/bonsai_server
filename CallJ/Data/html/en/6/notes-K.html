Grammar Point 1: The volitional verb form

The volitional verb form expresses the speaker's desire to perform an action, and can be used to make suggestions or invite others to join the speaker in an activity. Similar to other verb forms, each of the three groups of verbs has different rules for constructing the volitional form. As with other forms, the volitional form for group 1 and 2 verbs can be produced by regular rules, but the forms for group 3 don't follow any particular pattern.

1.1 Plain Volitional Form

GroupDictionary FormVolitionalEnglish
1行く行こうLet's go
飲む飲もうLet's drink
遊ぶ遊ぼうLet's play
2寝る寝ようLet's sleep
食べる食べようLet's eat
見る見ようLet's watch
3来る来ようLet's come
するしようLet's do

Group 1: The volitional form of the verb is formed by removing the final "u" sound from the dictionary form and replacing it with "ou".

Group 2: The volitional form of the verb is formed by dropping the final "ru" sound from the dictionary form and replacing it with "you".

Group 3: The volitional form of the verb doesn't follow any particular rule and must be learnt.

GroupPolite FormVolitional

3来ます来ましょう
しまうしましょう

As you can see from the table above, the polite form is simple to create for all groups of verbs. Simply replace the "masu" sound from the polite form with "mashou".

1.3 Suggestions and Invitations

Statements with the volitional form of the verb express the speaker's desire to perform an action, so they can be used as suggestions to another person. This form emphasizes the speaker's desire. Adding the question marker か softens this desire somewhat and turns the suggestion into an invitation.

Statement
自転車で銀行に行きましょう - "Let's go to the bank by bike."
リンゴを食べましょう - "Let's eat some apples."
Question
自転車で銀行に行きましょうか - "Shall we go the the bank by bike?"
リンゴを食べましょうか - "Shall we eat some apples?"
