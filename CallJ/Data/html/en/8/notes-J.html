Key Forms:

The following examples show the key forms to be practised in this lesson.
1Permission vs Forbidden
窓を開けてもいいですか"Do you mind if I open the window?"(Permission)
ここで煙草を吸ってはいけません"No smoking here"(Forbidden)



Grammar Point 1: Giving and Asking for Permission

For sentences that give permission to perform an action, you change the verb into the "te" form and add the marker "mo" plus the phrase "ii desu". This is equivalent to the expressions "You may..." or "You can..." in English. You can ask permission to perform an action by adding the question marker "ka" to the statement form.

Statement
コーヒーを飲んでもいいです - "You may drink coffee."
この本を読んでもいいです - "You can read this book."
Question
テレビを見てもいいですか - "Do you mind if I watch TV?"
ここでたばこを吸ってもいいですか - "May I smoke here?"

Grammar Point 2: Forbidding Actions

An expression similar to the one above is used to forbid an action. In this case, the grammar pattern is the "te" form of the verb followed by the topic marker "ha" plus the word "ikemasen".

Statement
窓を開けてはいけません - "You may not open the window."
ここで車を止めてはいけません - "You cannot park your car here."

This form can also be turned into a question by adding the question marker "ka". However, in this case the nuance is slightly different. Questions of this form express the speaker's assumption that the listener will not give permission for the action.

Question
ここでたばこを吸ってはいけませんか - "Am I not allowed to smoke here?"
ビールを飲んではいけませんか - "Am I not allowed to drink beer?"
