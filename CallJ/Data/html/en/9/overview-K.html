Expressing reasons and giving explanations


In this lesson we will practice how to express reasons and explanations. First, we will look at から and ので , which are used to mark a conditional clause as a reason. When these expressions connect two clauses, the first becomes the reason for the second. When a speaker ends a sentence with the のです or the plain form んだ , it indicates that he is giving or asking for an explanation. The explanation is usually based on information that the speaker and listener share. This grammar pattern can also be used to emphasise a point emotionally. Study the following examples.
1Reasons with Kara
あついからこーらをのむ。Since it's hot, I drink cola.
2Reasons with Node
じょんはいそがしいのでさっかーをしない。John won't play soccer because he's busy.
にちようびなのできょうかいへいく。Because it's Sunday, I'll go to church.
3Explanation with N-da/desu
じょんのかばんはおおきいんです。John's bag is big.
あすじょんにあうんです。I'll meet John tomorrow.
ゆうはんはぴざなんです。We're having pizza for dinner.
