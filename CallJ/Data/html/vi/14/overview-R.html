Expressing intentions and desires

In this lesson, you will practise how to express intentions and desires. We will consider a new verb form, the "tai" form, which is used to express whether or not a speaker intends to perform an action. This form can be used in both statements and questions, but we will also examine how to use its positive and negative forms, as well as its past and non-past forms. We will also see how this form changes depending on whether the sentence is polite or plain. The forms you will be expected to know to complete this lesson are summarised below.

1Want To Do
watashi wa nihongo o naraitaiI want to learn Japanese.
watashi wa tarou to toshokan e ikitaiI want to go to the library with Taro.
2Plan To Do
watashi wa chichi to daidokoro de asagohan wo tsukuru tsumoridesuI am planning to make breakfast with my father in the kitchen.
otouto wa niwa ni ki wo ueru tsumori datta.My younger brother was planning to plant a tree in the garden.
3Want
watashi wa tsumetai ko-ra ga hoshiiI want some cold cola.
4Want Someone To Do
watashi wa imouto ni enpitsu o katte hosiidesuI want my younger sister to buy some pencils.
ani ni rajio o kashite hosikattadesuI wanted you to lend my older brother your radio.
* Note: All answers given in this lesson should be of the form(s) shown above.
