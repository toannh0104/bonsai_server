sou shitara iidesuYou can do that.
zenbu anata no omou you ni sitara iidesuYou can do it all however you please.

The sentences you create in this lesson should all be similar to the forms shown above. The grammar required to form these sentences is covered in the text below.

Grammar Point 2: Expressing obligations

To express that someone must do something, the following expression is used. It corresponds to the expressions "must" or "have to" in English, and is used to describe both external obligations placed on the subject by someone else or the speaker's convictions about what needs to be done. To form this expression, change the plain negative form of the verb into the "ba" form and add the negative form of the verb "naru".

Grammar Point 3: Giving permission and making suggestions

Finally, we will look at another expression used to give permission. This expression gives permission to do the specified action and suggests that it will be enough for the situation. It carries the nuance that other choices are also acceptable. It is similar to the expressions "You can just..." or "It will be fine if you..." in English.

Remember that a sentence that uses the "te" form of the verb followed by "mo ii desu" gives permission to do the specified action. In this case, the focus is on the permission only. In contrast, a sentence using the "tara" form of a verb followed by "ii desu" is more of a suggestion that implies permission. This expression is often used when giving advice or recommending a course of action.
