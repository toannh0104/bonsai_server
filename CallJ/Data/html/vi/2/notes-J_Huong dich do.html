Các mẫu ngữ pháp cơ bản:

Bên dưới là các ví dụ về mẫu ngữ pháp cơ bản mà chúng ta sẽ thực hành trong bài học này.

1Statement vs Question:
私はテレビを見ます"I will watch TV."(Statement)
テレビを見ますか"Will you watch TV?"(Question)
2Positive vs Negative:
鈴木さんは来ます"Suzuki will come."(Positive)
鈴木さんは来ません"Suzuki will not come."(Negative)
3Non-Past vs Past:
私は今日東京へ行きます"I will go to Tokyo today."Non-past
私は昨日東京へ行きました"I went to Tokyo yesterday."(Past)
4Polite vs Plain:
私は手紙を書きます"I will write a letter."(Polite)
私は手紙を書く"I will write a letter."(Plain)
私は手紙を書きました"I wrote a letter."(Polite)
私は手紙を書いた"I wrote a letter."(Plain)

Các bạn hãy đặt câu tương tự với các câu mẫu bên trên. Dưới đây là phần giải thích chi tiết về ngữ pháp dùng trong các câu mẫu này.



Điểm ngữ pháp 1: Động từ

Có 3 nhóm động từ và một động từ thay đổi tùy thuộc vào nhóm của động từ đó. Có vô số động từ nhóm 1 và 2, There are 3 groups of verbs, and the manner in which a verb changes form depends on which group it is in. There are numerous group 1 and group 2 verbs, and within each group the rules for expanding the verbs may be easily defined. Group 3 consists of those few verbs that do not follow the rules so closely, and are dealt with separately.

In this lesson we will be using verbs in the polite and plain (dictionary) form, and will be considering the verb in both positive and negative forms. We will also be using the verb in its past and non-past forms. Thus, by considering the combinations of the above choices, we are looking at 8 possible forms. The following sections cover each form in turn, showing how the form is constructed for each verb group, giving examples.

1.1 Dictionary Form

The plain, non-negative, non-past form of a verb may be known as its dictionary form. It is in this form that we will give verbs in the dictionary, and it is also this form that a hint for a verb will display before displaying the verbs true form (for a given sentence). The following table shows a few examples of verbs in the dictionary form across the 3 groups:

Grammar Point 2: Objects, Places, etc.

2.2 The Object

The direct object of a transitive verb is always marked by the object marker を (pronounced お). For example:

私はテレビを見ます - "I will watch TV."

今朝コーヒーを飲みました - "I drank coffee this morning."

Transitive verbs take both a subject (which performs the action) and an object (which is acted upon), but the subject may be omitted if it has been mentioned earlier in the conversation or can be understood from context.

2.3 The Place

2.3.1 The Place where an Action is Performed

The location marker で is used to show the place where an action is performed in sentences with a verb.

図書館で勉強しています - "I am studying at the library."

Note that there are some restrictions on when "de" can be used. It can not be used in simple sentences that express the existence of a person or object with "iru" or "aru", or when expressing where a person lives with the verb "sumu". In these cases, the direction marker "ni" must be used.

Examples:

鈴木さんは京都にいます - "Suzuki is in Kyoto."

東京に住んでいますか - "Do you live in Tokyo?"

These special cases will be explained more in later lessons.

2.3.2 The Place as a Direction

The direction markers に and へ (pronounced え) are used to show the direction or destination of a movement verb such as "to come" or "to go". In this usage, に and へ are generally equivalent and either one can be used.

明日私は東京に行きます - "I will go to Tokyo tomorrow."

田中さんは学校に行きます - "Tanaka will go to school."
