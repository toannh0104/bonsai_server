Probability and uncertainty

In this lesson we will practise how to express varying degrees of uncertainty in sentences. When describing an uncertain event in Japanese, the expression to be used is decided by the information available, the source of that information, and how the speaker feels about the validity of that information. Many of the expressions can have similar English translations, so it is important to understand the nuances of each one.

1Impression, Guessing
asu no tesuto ha muzukashi sou desuTomorrow's test seems difficult.
2Expectation
tom ha taishikan ni itta hazu daTom must have gone to the embassy.
3Appearance
imanishisan no ie ha shizukana you desuImanishi's house appears to be quiet.
4Appearance, Similarity
tom ha gakusei mitai desu Tom looks like a student.
5Conjecture
asu ha hare deshouIt will probably be sunny tomorrow.
6Possibility
tom ha sakka wo suru kamoshirenaiTom might play soccer.
7Embedded question
kono orenge juice ha oishii ka douka wakaranaiI don't know whether this orange juice is delicious or not.
