How to express the concept of giving and receiving

In this lesson we will look at how to form sentences that convey the meaning of giving and receiving. There are a number of different verbs that are used for this - which verb should be used depends on the situation, the focus (subject) of the sentence, and the relative seniority level between the two people. Please refer to the notes for further information. The following table shows the kind of answers you will be expected to give throughout the lesson:

1Giving vs Receiving
トムさん は サムさん に リンゴ を あげました"Tom gave Sam an apple."
サムさん は わたし に リンゴ を くれました"Sam gave me an apple."
サムさん は トムさん に リンゴ を もらいました"Sam received an apple from Tom."
2Polite vs Plain
ジョンさん は サリーさん に はな を あげます"John will give Sally a flower." (polite)
ジョンさん は サリーさん に はなをあげる"John will give Sally a flower." (plain)
ジョンさん は サリーさん に はな を あげました"John gave Sally a flower." (polite)
ジョンさん は サリーさん に はなをあげた"John gave Sally a flower." (plain)
3Non-past vs Past
せんせい に プレゼント を あげます"I will give the teacher a present." (non-past)
せんせい に プレゼント を あげました"I gave the teacher a present." (past)
* Note: All answers given in this lesson should be of the form(s) shown above.
* Please refer to the notes for more information of the grammar structures that are used in this lesson.
