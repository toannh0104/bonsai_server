"I think I can go tomorrow."
Expressions with subordinate clauses


This lesson focuses on expressions that use subordinate clauses marked by the particle "と". You can think of subordinate clauses as small sentences that are embedded in a larger sentence. We will practice expressions that use a subordinate clause to express what someone says or thinks. We will also learn an expression that describes or explains a noun without using a subordinate clause.
1Quotation, Thought(引用)
行かないと言ったI said not to go
野球をすると思うI think to play baseball　
明日行けると思いますI think I can go tomorrow.
妹が好きだと言ったMy younger sister said she likes it.　
兄と行くと言ったI said I'll go with my older brother.
弟がほしいと思いますI think my younger brother will want it
野球をすると言うI'll say we will play baseball.
あそこへ行こうと思いますI'm thinking about going over there.
野球をしてみたいと思うI want to try playing baseball.
姉は勉強をすると思うI think my older sister will study.
2Description(標識化)
京都大学と言う大学the university,called Kyoto University
ポチと言う犬the dog, called pochi
大使館という建物the building called the embassy
