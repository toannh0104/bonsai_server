"I think I can go tomorrow."
Expressions with subordinate clauses


This lesson focuses on expressions that use subordinate clauses marked by the particle "と". You can think of subordinate clauses as small sentences that are embedded in a larger sentence. We will practice expressions that use a subordinate clause to express what someone says or thinks. We will also learn an expression that describes or explains a noun without using a subordinate clause.
1Quotation, Thought(引用)
いかないといったI said not to go
やきゅうをするとおもうI think to play baseball　
あすいけるとおもいますI think I can go tomorrow.
いもうとがすきだといったMy younger sister said she likes it.　
あにといくといったI said I'll go with my older brother.
おとうとがほしいとおもいますI think my younger brother will want it
やきゅうをするというI'll say we will play baseball.
あそこへいこうとおもいますI'm thinking about going over there.
やきゅうをしてみたいとおもうI want to try playing baseball.
あねはべんきょうをするとおもうI think my older sister will study.
2Description(標識化)
きょうとだいがくというだいがくthe university,called Kyoto University
ぽちといういぬthe dog, called pochi
たいしかんというたてものthe building called the embassy
