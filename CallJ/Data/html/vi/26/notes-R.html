Key Forms:

The following examples show the key forms to be practised in this lesson.

1Even though
hareteiru noni tenisu wo shimasenEven though it's sunny outside, we won't play tennis.
taro ha himana noni gakkou he ikimasenEven though Taro has nothing to do, he doesn't go to school.
kyoto ha hare na noni osaka ha ame daEven though the weather is clear in Kyoto, it's raining in Osaka.
taro ha isogashii noni chikoku suruEven though Taro is busy, he's late.
2Even if
taro ni sugaku ni tsuite kiitemo mudada Even if you ask Taro about math, it's no use.
koko de netemo iiYou can sleep here.

The sentence you create in this lesson should all be similar to the forms shown above. The grammar required to form these sentences is covered in the text below.


Grammar Point 1: Unexpected outcomes (AのにB)

This grammar point connects two sentences and shows that the result in sentence B is the opposite of what the speaker expects from the established facts or current state described in sentence A. In other words, sentence B is the opposite of what the speaker reasonably expects given that sentence A is true. This grammar pattern is used in much the same way as the expression "even though ..." or "although ..." in English.

夕べ早く寝たのに、今日は眠い - "Even though I went to bed early last night, I'm sleepy today."

雨が降ってるのに、太郎は傘を持って来ませんでした - "Even though it's raining, Taro didn't bring an umbrella."

Sentence A is always in the plain form. Recall that verbs and "i" adjectives do not need any special endings for the plain form, but nouns and "na" adjectives must be followed by the copula だ . However, because sentence A is followed by の it becomes a subordinate clause, and thus the copula だ changes into な for "na" adjectives and nouns. See the examples below.

太郎は日本人なのに、魚が嫌いだ - "Even though Taro is Japanese, he doesn't like fish."

弟は暇なのに、全然勉強しません - "Even though my little brother has free time, he doesn't study at all."

This pattern is not used when both sentence A and sentence B describe deliberate actions by the speaker. However, sentence A can be a deliberate action if sentence B describes some external event that prevents it from being completed, as shown in the example below.

ジュースを飲みたかったのに、売り切れてしまいました - "I wanted to drink some juice, but it sold out."

Also note that sentence B cannot be a question, request, or command.

Grammar Point 2: "Even if ..." (AてもB)

This grammar pattern shows that sentence B holds even if the condition given in sentence A is true. It is usually translated as "even if ..." or "although" . The verb of sentence A must be in the "te" form followed by も . If sentence A originally used the copula だ , then the copula is replaced by でも .

入学試験が簡単でも妹は合格しない - "Even if the entrance exam is easy, my little sister won't pass."

先生に聞いても分かりませんでした - "Although I asked the teacher, I still didn't understand."

たくさん食べてもお腹が空く - "I get hungry even if I eat a lot."

Grammar Point 2.1 Asking for permission

In a previous lesson, we studied how to give and request permission using the "te" form of a verb plus もいい . This expression is actually an idiomatic usage of grammar point 2.

煙草を吸ってもいいですか - "May I smoke?"

ここで寝てもいい - "You can sleep here."
