Compulsion, permission, and requests with the causative form


In this lesson we will look at the construction and use of the causative form. This is typically used to convey the meaning of compulsion, i.e. someone being compelled to do something. However, the same form may also be used to convey someone being allowed to do a particular action. Also, combined with "Kudasai" (please), it may be used to ask permission to do an action.

1Type
watashi ha kodomo ni yasai wo tabesasemashita"I made the child eat vegetables" (compulsion)
watashi ha kodomo ni okashi wo tabesasemashita"I allowed the child to eat sweets" (permission)
karada ga warui node, hayaku kaerasetekudasai"I feel bad, so please let me go home early" (request)
2Statement vs Question
kyouju ha gakusei ni repo-to wo kakasemasu"The professor makes the students to write a report" (Statement)
kyouju ha gakusei ni repo-to wo kakasemasuka"Does the professor make the students to write a report?"(Question)
3Non-past vs Past
butyou ha tanakasan ni toukyou he ikasemasu"The director makes tanakasan to go to Tokyo"(non-past)
butyou ha tanakasan ni toukyou he ikasemashita"The director made tanakasan to go to Tokyo" (past)
4Polite vs Plain
watashi ha imouto ni benkyousasemasu"I make my sister study"(polite)
watashi ha imouto ni benkyousaseru"I make my sister study" (plain)
