Further Adjectives

In this lesson we will practise expressing simple feelings about objects with adjectives. Two forms of adjective use will be practiced. First we will learn a form called suki1, where the adjective is located at the end of the sentence and describes feelings about one noun. We will also consider a related form called suki2 where the adjective refers to an activity described by a simple sentence. To show how adjectives can modify verbs as adverbs, we'll look at sentences expressing changes in the size or number of objects. Finally, we will also learn the past form for each kind of adjective introduced in this lesson.

1suki1 vs suki2 vs adj:
私はサッカーが好きです"I like soccer." (suki1)
私はサッカーをするのが好きです"I like playing soccer." (suki2)
リンゴは大きくなりました"The apple became bigger." (adj)
2Positive vs Negative:
私はお酒が好きです"I like alcohol."
私はお酒が好きではありません"I don't like alcohol."
3Present vs Past:
私はサッカーが好きでした"I liked soccer." (suki1)
私はサッカーをするのが好きでした"I liked to play soccer." (suki2)
リンゴは大きくなりませんでした"The apple didn't become bigger." (suki1)
