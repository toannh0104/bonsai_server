The sentences you create in this lesson should all be similar to the forms shown above. The grammar required to form these sentences is covered in the text below.



Grammar Point 1: The volitional verb form

The volitional verb form expresses the speaker's desire to perform an action, and can be used to make suggestions or invite others to join the speaker in an activity. Similar to other verb forms, each of the three groups of verbs has different rules for constructing the volitional form. As with other forms, the volitional form for group 1 and 2 verbs can be produced by regular rules, but the forms for group 3 don't follow any particular pattern.

1.3 Suggestions and Invitations

Statements with the volitional form of the verb express the speaker's desire to perform an action, so they can be used as suggestions to another person. This form emphasizes the speaker's desire. Adding the question marker ‚© softens this desire somewhat and turns the suggestion into an invitation.
