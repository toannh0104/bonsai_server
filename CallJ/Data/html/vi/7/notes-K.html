1.2 The "te" form of verbs

The positive requests above are constructed by creating a sentence describing the action to be performed, changing the verb to a form called the "te" form, and adding the word "kudasai" after the verb. The "te" form of verbs is used to form compound sentences and shows up in many other grammar patterns which will be covered by later lessons.

The "te" form is formed by first changing a verb its plain past tense. If the plain past tense ends with a "ta" sound, change that sound to "te". Otherwise, if the plain past tense ends with a "da" sound, change that sound to "de". Unlike most other verb forms, the group that the verb falls into does not influence how the "te" form is made.

1.3 The "naide" form of verbs

The negative requests above are constructed in the same way as the positive requests, except that a different form of the verb is used. This form is called the "naide" form, and expresses that the action indicated by the verb is not done. In all cases it is formed by simply adding the sound "de" to the negative non-past form of a verb.
